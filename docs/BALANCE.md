# Balance / playtest notes

Generated by the dev harness `game/tools/balance_report.tscn`, which plays the full week
under several player strategies and prints the resulting hidden states + which ending fires.
Re-run any time after content changes:

```bash
godot --headless --path game tools/balance_report.tscn
```

## Baseline reading (PR-13, 46-card week)

| Strategy | cards resolved | connection | security | partnership | wellbeing | standing | Ending |
|----------|----------------|-----------|----------|-------------|-----------|----------|--------|
| **warm** (always the tender option) | 70 | +51 | +42 | −5 | −10 | −27 | *They'll remember the stories.* |
| **curt** (always the short option) | 142 | −62 | −75 | +6 | +4 | +15 | *They got quieter this week.* |
| **balanced** (first reachable) | 72 | +45 | +44 | −5 | −10 | −25 | *They'll remember the stories.* |
| **defer everything** | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | *Enough.* |

### What this confirms (the arithmetic works)
- Playstyles produce **distinct, thematically-correct** end-states and **different endings**.
- Pouring into the child (warm) buys deep connection/security at the cost of self (wellbeing
  −10), work (standing −27), and partner (−5). The trade-off is real and legible.
- Snapping through the day (curt) is *cheap in time* — a curt player processes ~2× the cards
  (142 vs 70) because dismissals cost little — but connection/security collapse. Thematically
  apt: you get through the day by getting through your child.
- Deferring everything costs nothing and changes nothing → the neutral default ending.

### Tuning notes (for a human playtest, not blind changes)
1. **Connection endings dominate by priority.** Warm play ends at wellbeing −10 yet still lands
   the connection ending because `the_stories` (connection ≥ 6) outranks `held_together`
   (wellbeing ≤ −4). If we want self-erasure to surface even amid a strong bond, raise
   `held_together`'s priority or add a compound match (high connection AND low wellbeing →
   "you gave them everything; there was nothing left for you"). Left as-is pending playtest.
2. **`balanced` ≈ `warm`** here only because the first-listed response is usually the tender
   one; it is a harness artifact, not a player behavior. A real mixed player lands between the
   warm and curt columns.
3. Reserves rarely bottom out under warm/balanced (energy 6 / patience 5 left) because overnight
   refills are generous; curt play keeps reserves high too. The L3 burnout feel is reachable
   (stress autoplay hits it) but a *careful* player may not see it — intended, but worth a
   human read on whether late-week should bite harder.

These are intentional design dials, not defects. The harness makes re-checking them a one-liner.
